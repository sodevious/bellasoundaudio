Unveiling the Music

About Bella Sound

The essence of Bella Sound is in its name: Beautiful Sound. It is a symphony of both science and art uniquely combined to remove the veil from recorded music playback. This was the dream and brainchild of Mike Vice for 50 years, and it is now a reality.

It started for Mike in his teens when he began designing speakers and amplifiers. It continued through a Physics degree from UC Berkeley and an Electrical Engineering degree from Stanford University. Meanwhile he was on his way to accumulating 40 patents in microwave engineering at Watkins-Johnson Co., Hewlett Packard Co., Agilent Technologies, Avago Technologies, and Broadcom.

Throughout this path, audio remained his first and foremost love, and he determined to use his physics background and microwave experience to enable a raw, candid, and unveiled presentation of recorded music. This resulted in a design philosophy that emphasizes simplicity and elegance, putting audio signal integrity above all else.

Now retired from microwave engineering, he has devoted himself to actualizing on the playground of greatest primal importance â audio playback. The result is Bella Sound.

Design Philosophy

The Bella Sound approach is to develop platforms that support the full demands of playback audio while suppressing every mechanism that detracts from musical beauty and realism.

This starts with architectural designs that support the power, attack, bandwidth, and coherence of audio signals while suppressing non-linearity, noise, timbre coloration, and delay dispersion.

While this sounds like textbook engineering, Bella Sound does not stop here. We tap into a rich legacy of audio engineering art with strong theory/art interchange, together with countless hours of auditioning to achieve the exquisitely unveiled and pristine listening room result that sounds more like a reenactment of the music than a mere reproduction of it.
